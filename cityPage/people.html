Community

Though Lethbridge is a predominantly English-speaking, same as many of Alberta’s other municipalities, the city’s population changes year-round, and as such the community is often incredibly welcoming and willing to help newcomers become adjusted to the small city’s hustle and bustle. Students from across the world spend autumn, winter, and spring studying at Lethbridge’s two major post-secondary institutions. This brings a diverse crowd of people from various cultures and backgrounds to experience what the prairies have to offer.

Alongside that, the city has strong ties to Indigenous and Asian cultures, being located next to the Blood Tribe Reserve on Treaty 7 land, alongside having a historic Japanese presence dating back a century. Several of the city’s arts and cultural hubs, such as the Galt Museum, Southern Alberta Art Gallery, and Nikka Yuko Japanese Garden offer exhibits and events exploring the histories of these cultures. Many of these cultures can also be explored online when closed or out-of-season. Though their presence isn’t overwhelming, you can find other various traces of these cultures around the city, from the greetings to a vast array of small shops dedicated to clothing, jewelry, décor, and especially food.
