Attractions

Although Lethbridge provides a comfortable and quiet atmosphere, there’s still plenty to do, from exploring various tourist attractions and public spaces to participating in frequently hosted events across the city. The Nikka Yuko Japanese Garden combines the wondrous nature and Japanese culture that can be found in the city into one place, and although the gardens may not be open year-round, they offer a variety of programs and community events like Tea Thursdays, Craft Fridays, Anime Saturdays, tea ceremonies, and other opportunities to experience and engage with Japanese culture. Alongside that, the Galt Museum offers a vast look into the city’s history with exhibits ranging from its humble origins as a coal mining town to our Indigenous roots with Blackfoot language classes, with Fort Whoop Up directly next door to it in the river bottom. Several organizations also host exhibits, resources, and workshops for local artists, including the Galt Museum, Southern Alberta Art Gallery (SAAG) and Casa, spanning everything from up-and-coming painters and photographers to local legends and the truly passionate creators.

With a focus on making these destinations easily accessible, a new Lethbridge Transit program was launched during the summer of 2022, offering a free-access ride across the city with the CultureLINK line, spanning Nikka Yuko to the Helen Schuler Nature Center, stopping at each of the major destinations listed in this section, which will hopefully be making a return in future years. Several events are also hosted in the Galt Gardens, as well as the newly made Festival Square located on 3rd avenue, both of which are right beside SAAG, celebrating cultural keystones in the community with several festivals scheduled year-round, alongside simple events like movie nights.
