Nature & Parks

Located in Canada’s western prairies, Lethbridge is squarely placed between several natural marvels and other Albertan towns and cities, making it an easy hub to experience the wonders of the vast plains, foothills, and Rocky mountains, being just two hours south of Calgary and 1 hour from the U.S. border. The Old Man River provides several parks and nature reserves to explore, such as Popson and Cottonwood Parks, alongside the Elizabeth Hall Wetlands, a small looping trail off Highway 3 bordered by a gorgeous cottonwood forest and lake where beavers, muskrats, and western painted turtles make their homes. Across the river, the Helen Schuler Nature Centre offers several exhibits, programs, and events to learn more about the coulees and the flora and fauna that inhabit it for every age demographic.

If winding hikes through forests and foothills isn’t your cup of tea, several smaller parks are scattered throughout the city such as Nicholas Sheran and Henderson Lake Parks. These places offer short but sweet slices of nature connecting to residential neighbourhoods, often only taking a few minutes to reach on foot. To top it all off, trips in and out of the city are surrounded by Alberta’s vast plains, where the breathtaking patchwork crops of canola, corn, and pulses envelop the city’s surrounding prairies in a natural quilt of beauty and wonder.
